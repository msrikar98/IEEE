Upcoming Events

The IEEE Student-Teacher and Research Engineer/Scientist (STAR) Program was developed to address the growing concern that, the young generation or youth especially, girls are discouraged from careers in mathematics, science, and engineering.

This educational outreach program promotes involvement of IEEE members with local junior and high schools in order to create a clear picture of what engineering careers means. Through a one-to-one interaction between WIE volunteers and a Student Team, the main aim of this program is to create a technical support network among the students for the future budding engineers which INDIA will produce.

The "WOMEN IN ENGINEERING" STAR PROGRAM was organized in a successful manner on 23rd of August, 2012. It was the first session of the STAR program, in which a presentation was given to create awareness among the students of 8th to 12th standard students Shravanthi Public School, Gannavaram. We had a splendid response for this event.

Event date: 12 - January - 2013

In the world of emerging technologies, automation of devices plays a prominent role in not only reducing the efforts required from human but also gives accurate results with effective time saving strategies. One of the techniques to automate the devices is by applying FUZZY LOGIC control to it. There exists large variety of design and implementation in it.

IEEE-KLU Student Branch organized a tech talk on Fuzzy Logic Control - its Features and Future. Mrs.S.V.N.L.Lalitha (Assoc Prof) in EEE Department of KLUniversity presided over the event. She had spoken on the features of Fuzzy Logic Control showing examples of the fuzzy logic applications and an introduction to the tool box. Mrs.S.V.N.L.Lalitha emphasized the importance of "FUZZY LOGIC" and wide use of its applications. Systematic framework to process vague variable and knowledge the various automation tasks is discussed with regard to the sources.

This technical lecture is aimed at empowering student's knowledge on fuzzy logic and to converse about the techniques that are often integrated with other Computational Intelligence methods, and with other computational techniques, giving rise to powerful computational approaches & describing about latest technological development using this FUZZY LOGIC control and effective use of it.

The session was quite interactive and interesting. There has been a good turnaround of students on cite. students have given a positive response about the event and are quiet intrigued about the methods adapted by industries to automate their process.

Event Name: INDUSTRIAL VISITS-I
Event date: : : 10-February-2013
Place: : S.V.R. ELECTRICALS
Students Participated :31

To bridge the gap between theoretical aspects what students learn in classrooms and practical approach done in industries IEEE KLU SB has organized an industrial visit to S.V.R. ELECTRICALS which enhances the pragmatic knowledge of students.

S.V.R. ELECTRICALS is renowned for manufacturing transformer in coastal region of AP and also engaged in manufacturing Power, Distribution & Special application type of transformers. Students were explained about the different types of transformer available for various applications and utilities. Construction of winding, core and finally assembling them to form a transformer is authentically described. Trainer`s knowledge has helped us a lot in understanding possible methods of maintaining insulation between windings and other conducting material of the transformer lucidly. Purity of oil is an important factor to cool the transformer simultaneously; this crude oil is brought to the industry and refined to eliminate minute particles which may cause breakdown of transformer occasionally. Different temperature chambers at which oil is passed and improves its insulation capability was enunciated to the participants.

We were glad to be shown about various testing procedures of transformer like open circuit test, short circuit test, high voltage test, insulation test, double frequency test, heat run test, resistance and breakdown voltage test. This exposure will make students to comprehend their peer knowledge on transformers. Students were cautioned about the high end results for improper manufacturing, which leads to short circuit to windings and loss of entire equipment. transformers are designed even to withstand fluctuation in voltage levels.

These industrial visits will encompass the higher learning capability, effective interaction with industry & to know about updating technology in industry for student.
